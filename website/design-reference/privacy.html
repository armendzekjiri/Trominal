Privacy

The shortest privacy policy you'll read this year.

Trominal is self-hosted and ships with no telemetry. There is, in the literal sense, almost nothing to disclose. Last updated May 6, 2026.

TL;DR

Trominal is an SSH client. It runs on your machine, talks to a server you control, and connects to hosts you've configured. We do not collect or transmit any data about your usage of the app. No analytics, no usage pings, no anonymous identifiers, no crash reports unless you opt in.

If that's all you needed to know, you can close this tab. If you want the details:

What we collect

We — meaning the Trominal project maintainers operating trominal.dev — collect the following:

  • This website's server logs. Standard request logs (IP, user-agent, path, timestamp) retained for 14 days, used to investigate abuse and outages. We don't have analytics installed.
  • Communication you initiate. If you email us, file an issue, or join the Discord, we read those messages. Obviously.

That's the entire list.

What we don't collect

  • Your hosts, addresses, fingerprints, or any SSH config.
  • Your snippets, terminal output, or shell history.
  • Your identities, private keys, or certificates.
  • App usage events of any kind. There is no "anonymous" telemetry — there is no telemetry at all.
  • Your IP address from inside the app. The desktop and mobile clients do not phone home.

Your self-hosted server

When you run a Trominal server (ghcr.io/trominal/server) inside your own infrastructure, that server stores:

  • Hosts, host groups, and tags you've configured.
  • Snippets you and your team have created.
  • Encrypted identity blobs (sealed client-side with your master password before upload — the server cannot read them).
  • Per-user audit logs of mutations: who added a host, who shared a snippet, who connected and when. Configurable retention; default 90 days.

This data lives on disk wherever you've mounted the data volume. The maintainers cannot see it, access it, or recover it for you. That's the point.

AI providers

If you turn on AI assistance, prompts and the terminal context you opt to share are sent to whatever provider you've configured — Anthropic, OpenAI, or your own LLM endpoint. Trominal is not in that path. Requests go directly from your client to the provider; we never see them.

By default, AI is off and "send terminal context" is off. Redaction rules strip common secret formats (private keys, AWS keys, JWTs, hex-encoded tokens) before any data leaves the device.

Update checks

The desktop client checks for updates by fetching a static JSON manifest from releases.trominal.dev. The request includes only the standard headers any HTTP client sends. We don't log it, we don't analyze it, and you can disable update checks in Settings → Connection.

Crash reports

If the app crashes, you'll see a dialog asking whether to send the crash report. Off by default. If you opt in, we receive a stack trace, app version, and OS version — no terminal contents, no hostnames, no identifying information from the app's data.

Cookies on this site

This website (trominal.dev) sets zero cookies and includes zero third-party scripts. The only fonts are loaded from Google Fonts, which has its own policies. If you want to avoid even that, the source is on GitHub — fork it and self-host the fonts.
